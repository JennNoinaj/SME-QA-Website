--- layout: article section: about permalink: /about/authority/ title: The authority for SME-QA intro: In March 2019, the United States Digital Services (USDS) and OPM partnered to pilot a Technical Subject Matter Expert (SME) Hurdled Assessment process prior to the category ranking of applicants and placement on the selection certificate. The primary objective of this pilot was to enhance the quality of hires through a partnership between the agency hiring manager, human resources specialist, and SMEs using a documented multiple hurdle assessment process. ---

{{ page.intro }}

Here is the authority agencies have to use this process:

In OPM's Delegated Examining Operations Handbook:

  • Pages 5-23 and 5-24 detail using multiple assessments where applicants can be cut after each stage and preference is only applied to those who pass in “Scenario 5: Multiple Assessments – Cumulative Score (using a passing score)”.
  • The footnote on page 4-2 details that when using a passing grade assessment, such as a structured interview, applicants should not get 70 points or preference after being found minimally qualified - being minimally qualified only gains applicants entry into the pass/fail interview.
  • Page 2-18 details that the self-assessment portion of the occupational questionnaire is just one of many assessments that could be used. Previous SME-QA pilots did not use the self-assessment portion of the occupational questionnaire.

In OPM's Structured Interview guidance:

  • Page 15 says that structured interviews may have one interviewer but may also have two to three on the panel. SME-QA pilots used one SME and one applicant on phone interviews but to mitigate any risks, we used structured interview questions, trained SMEs on interviews, and had SMEs transcribe the interview to the best of their ability.

In OPM's memorandum "Improving Federal Hiring through the Use of Effective Assessment Strategies to Advance Mission Outcomes":

  • Clarifies that it is not only legal but encouraged for HR to use SMEs to determine whether applicants are qualified.
  • Clarifies that SMEs may work with HR to identify and document the competencies and proficiency levels required to perform the essential functions of a job – a process commonly known as job analysis. Agencies should never cut and paste competencies from OPM's qualiflication standards. See also the Delegated Examining Operations Handbook page 4-23.
